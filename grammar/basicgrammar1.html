Basic English Sentence Structure (SVO)

The most common structure for simple English statements is Subject - Verb - Object (SVO). Understanding this basic order will help you form clear and correct sentences.


  • Subject: The person, place, thing, or idea that performs the action.
  • Verb: The action word.
  • Object: The person, place, thing, or idea that receives the action.

Understanding SVO

SVO Structure

Subject + Verb + Object

Examples:

  • The dog chased the cat.
  • She reads books.
  • He eats apples.
  • They play soccer.
  • My mother loves music.
