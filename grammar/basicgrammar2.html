Basic English Sentence Structure: Yes/No Questions

In English, we form simple yes/no questions by often changing the order of the subject and the first verb (usually a form of 'to be' or an auxiliary verb like 'do' or 'does'). This lesson will focus on these basic question structures.

Forming Yes/No Questions

Using 'to be'

Statement: Subject + am/is/are + (complement).

Question: Am/Is/Are + Subject + (complement)?

Examples with 'to be':

  • Statement: You are happy. → Question: Are you happy?
  • Statement: He is a teacher. → Question: Is he a teacher?
  • Statement: They are tired. → Question: Are they tired?

Using 'do/does'

Statement: Subject + (other verb).

Question: Do/Does + Subject + (base form of verb)?

Examples with 'do/does':

  • Statement: She likes coffee. → Question: Does she like coffee?
  • Statement: They play games. → Question: Do they play games?
  • Statement: He speaks Spanish. → Question: Does he speak Spanish?
